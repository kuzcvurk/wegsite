Michelle McBeth

 
 
 
 
 


So I didn’t win again, but I got a fair amount out of my campaign. For one thing, I did get a few people interested enough in my series to buy my first book. Here’s hoping for some good reviews.


I’m pleased that I wasn’t immediately rejected. Last time I got my no-thank-you notice in about three days. This time it was almost two weeks. I’d like to think that they were seriously considering it and not just all on vacation. Since I’ll never know for sure, I choose to believe the former.


I got about three times the page hits of my first campaign and an overwhelming 92% of those were people who simply found my book on KS and checked it out. Only 8% were people I sent the direct link to, begging for votes.


As I suspected, by biggest hits were the last few days of my campaign. That seems to be the case with the hot & trending list as well-books that are about to end their campaigns are the majority of the list. It makes sense, since you only get three votes.


I can’t bring myself to be too disappointed. I’m apparently not the target audience of most scouters, and to be honest, some of the books that get selected look pretty crappy. I understand KS does put you through a round or two of professional editing, but I guess if you’re starting from 50 Shades, it’s hard to improve to Scarlet Letter.


Oh well! Look for “The Lunar Conspiracy” in late August, 2017!
